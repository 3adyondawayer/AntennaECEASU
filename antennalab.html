🌊 Radiation Power Density W(θ,φ)

Power per unit area on a sphere of radius r. Always decreases as 1/r² regardless of direction — the inverse square law.

W(θ,φ) = dP / dA [W/m²] W(r,θ,φ) = U(θ,φ) / r² W_iso = P_rad / (4π r²) [isotropic]
Key idea: W depends on both direction (θ,φ) AND distance r. Double the distance → ¼ the power density (−6 dB).

💡 Radiation Intensity U(θ,φ)

Power per unit solid angle. Removes the 1/r² dependence — a purely directional antenna property independent of distance.

U(θ,φ) = r² · W(θ,φ) [W/sr] P_rad = ∬ U(θ,φ) sinθ dθ dφ U_avg = P_rad / 4π [isotropic equivalent]

🔵 Solid Angle Ω

3D extension of a planar angle. Measured in steradians (sr). Think of it as the "shadow area" on a unit sphere cast by a surface patch.

dΩ = sinθ dθ dφ [sr] Ω_sphere = ∫₀²π ∫₀π sinθ dθ dφ = 4π sr Half-sphere: 2π sr · Cone 45°: ≈ 1.84 sr

📐 Beam Solid Angle Ω_A

Solid angle the antenna would need if radiating uniformly at its peak intensity. A narrow beam → small Ω_A → high directivity.

Ω_A = ∬ F(θ,φ) dΩ [sr] where F = U/U_max P_rad = U_max · Ω_A (power = peak × beam)

🎯 Directivity D

Ratio of radiation intensity in a given direction to the spherical average. Purely geometric — no losses included.

D(θ,φ) = U(θ,φ) / U_avg = 4π·U / P_rad D_max = 4π / Ω_A D · Ω_A = 4π ← invariant — always true!
Reference values: Isotropic: D=1 (0 dBi) · Short dipole: D=1.5 (1.76 dBi) · Half-wave dipole: D=1.64 (2.15 dBi)

📶 Gain G

Directivity weighted by radiation efficiency η. Accounts for ohmic losses in the antenna material. G ≤ D always.

G(θ,φ) = η · D(θ,φ) (0 ≤ η ≤ 1) G_max = η · D_max G[dBi] = 10·log₁₀(G_max) (η=1 → G=D)

📖 How to Read Polar Plots

Each radial distance represents gain in that direction (normalized). The outer ring = 0 dB (peak). Inner rings are −3, −10, −20, −30 dB contours. The red dashed circle marks the −3 dB half-power points.

↔️ E-plane vs H-plane

The E-plane (φ=0) contains the electric field vector and the maximum radiation direction. The H-plane (φ=90°) is orthogonal. Both together define the full 3D pattern.

📐 Solid Angle Formula

Ω = ∫₀^φ_max ∫₀^θ_max sinθ dθ dφ = φ_max · (1 − cosθ_max) [sr] Full sphere: 4π ≈ 12.566 sr Half sphere: 2π ≈ 6.283 sr Cone θ=45°: ≈ 1.840 sr Cone θ=60°: ≈ 3.142 sr = π sr
Analogy: Just as a 2D angle θ = arc/radius, a solid angle Ω = area/r². A full sphere subtends 4π steradians at its center — this is why D_iso = 1 and D·Ω_A = 4π.

🌊 What is S = E × H?

The Poynting vector S gives the instantaneous power flow per unit area (W/m²). Its time-average equals the radiation power density W.

S = E × H [W/m²] ⟨S⟩ = ½ Re(E × H*) [time-avg] |⟨S⟩| = |E|² / (2η₀) [far field] η₀ = 120π ≈ 377 Ω [free space]

📡 Far-Field Geometry

In the far field (r ≫ λ): E ⊥ H ⊥ r̂, all mutually perpendicular. The wave looks locally planar.

E_θ ∝ F(θ,φ) · e^{−jkr} / r H_φ = E_θ / η₀ (TEM wave) S_r = |E_θ|² / (2η₀) ∝ F²(θ)/r²
